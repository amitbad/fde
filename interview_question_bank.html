The FDE Interview Question Bank

A consolidated drill of the highest-probability questions across every domain, plus the behavioral and scenario rounds that decide FDE offers. Each answer is a model you can say out loud. Practice by reading the question, answering aloud, then expanding the card to compare.

13.1 Behavioral & Consulting (Highest Weight for FDE)

FDE is customer-facing. These decide the loop as much as technical rounds. Use STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) and always quantify the result.

Tell me about a time you turned around a struggling customer/stakeholder relationship.
Testing: consulting maturity, ownership.
Use STAR: name the tension (missed expectations), the action (listened, reset scope, delivered a quick visible win, over-communicated), and a quantified result (renewed trust, expanded engagement). Emphasize proactive communication and under-promising/over-delivering.
You disagree with a customer's technical direction. What do you do?
Testing: influence without authority.
Seek to understand their constraints first, then present data/tradeoffs (cost, risk, maintainability) rather than opinion, offer a small proof to de-risk, and if they still choose their path, document the tradeoff and support them professionally. Being right matters less than the customer succeeding.
Describe explaining a deeply technical problem to a non-technical executive.
Testing: communication range.
Lead with business impact ("this is costing $X/month and risks the SLA"), use an analogy, avoid jargon, and end with a clear recommendation and decision needed. Show you can move between whiteboarding Spark internals and a CFO conversation.
How do you handle being dropped into an unfamiliar codebase/environment under time pressure?
Testing: the core FDE reality.
Establish a mental map fast (architecture, data flow, owners), find the highest-impact problem, deliver an early visible win to build credibility, and communicate a plan. Comfort with ambiguity and rapid ramp is the job.
A deliverable is slipping. How and when do you communicate?
Testing: trust management.
Early and proactively — the moment risk is real, not at the deadline. Give the cause, the revised plan, options/tradeoffs, and what I need from them. Silence destroys trust; transparent replanning preserves it.
Why do you want to be an FDE rather than a pure engineer?
Testing: role fit.
I like owning outcomes end-to-end with real customers — combining hands-on engineering with architecture and consulting, seeing my work adopted, and translating between technical and business worlds. The variety and impact suit me.

13.2 Scenario / System-Design Rounds

Design an end-to-end ingestion-to-BI platform for a new customer.
Testing: synthesizing the whole hub.
Auto Loader → bronze (streaming table) → silver (cleansed + expectations) → gold (aggregates) via DLT in a medallion layout; Unity Catalog for governance/lineage/masking; Jobs compute + Trigger.AvailableNow for cost; Terraform + DABs for reproducible deploys and CI/CD; system tables + tags for FinOps; Databricks SQL/BI on gold. Justify each choice by cost, security, and maintainability.
A nightly job runs 6 hours, sometimes OOMs, and 20 nodes didn't help. Fix it.
Testing: Ch 2–3 applied.
Spark UI first: adding nodes not helping screams skew, not capacity. Find the long-pole task, confirm skewed join key and/or spill, fix with AQE skew join / salting / broadcast of the small side, right-size partitions, enable Photon, and improve file layout (OPTIMIZE + liquid clustering). Verify tasks are balanced and downsize the cluster — usually big runtime and cost drops.
Departments share credentials; auditors failed them. Design the fix.
Testing: Ch 4 applied.
Unity Catalog metastore with storage credentials + external locations (no shared keys/mounts), group-based RBAC via identity provider, dynamic column masking + row filters for PII, and system.access.audit dashboards for who-queried-what. Result: least privilege, full audit trail, passing compliance.
Bill doubled overnight. Find and stop the bleed.
Testing: Ch 9 applied.
Query system.billing.usage × list_prices grouped by workload/team/date; typical causes are always-on All-Purpose clusters, missing autotermination, oversized warehouses, or a new skewed job. Apply the biggest lever (Jobs compute, autotermination, right-size), add cluster-policy guardrails and a cost dashboard to prevent recurrence.
Customer needs sub-minute fraud scoring. Batch or streaming? What stack?
Testing: matching architecture to SLA.
Real-time: Structured Streaming (or serving endpoint) with a low-latency feature lookup (Feature Store online store), model loaded by alias for champion/challenger, idempotent sink, watermark-bounded state. Contrast with the cheaper batch path I'd use if the SLA were nightly.
Migrate 500 legacy notebooks with hardcoded mounts and no tests to production standard.
Testing: Ch 4 + 7 applied.
Replace DBFS mounts with UC external locations, extract reusable logic into tested Python wheels (pytest), wrap pipelines in DABs, add CI/CD gates (validate/test/deploy) via GitHub Actions, and stage rollout dev→QA→prod with cluster policies. Prioritize by business value and de-risk incrementally.

13.3 Rapid-Fire Technical (Know Cold)

Control Plane vs Data Plane?
Control Plane = Databricks-managed UI/scheduling/metadata; Data Plane = where compute runs and data is processed (Classic in customer VPC, or Serverless).
Classic vs Serverless compute?
Classic runs VMs in the customer's cloud (minutes to start, customer-managed network); Serverless is Databricks-managed, near-instant, infra cost folded into the DBU rate.
Narrow vs wide transformation?
Narrow = no shuffle (filter/select); wide = shuffle + new stage (groupBy/join/distinct).
Job vs stage vs task?
Job = one action; stages split at shuffles (N shuffles = N+1 stages); task = one partition of a stage.
repartition vs coalesce?
repartition = full shuffle, rebalances, up or down; coalesce = reduce only, no shuffle, can be uneven.
Broadcast join — when?
When one side is small (~<100MB) so it's copied to every executor, avoiding a shuffle.
What causes spill and how do you see it?
Execution memory exhausted → data written to local disk. Spark UI stage detail Spill (Memory/Disk) columns.
Delta time travel?
Query past versions via VERSION AS OF / TIMESTAMP AS OF, backed by the transaction log; used for audits and rollback.
OPTIMIZE vs VACUUM?
OPTIMIZE compacts small files (and clusters); VACUUM deletes old unreferenced files past the retention window.
Liquid clustering vs partitioning + Z-Order?
Liquid clustering dynamically organizes by query columns without fixed partition keys, adapting as queries change; replaces brittle static partitioning + Z-Order.
Unity Catalog 3-level namespace?
catalog.schema.table, under a regional metastore.
Storage credential vs external location?
Credential = the IAM role/managed identity; external location = an authorized storage path using that credential.
Streaming table vs materialized view (DLT)?
Streaming table = process each record once (ingest); MV = recompute/refresh full result (aggregations).
What does SEQUENCE BY do?
Orders CDC events in APPLY CHANGES so late/out-of-order events never overwrite newer state.
Exactly-once in streaming?
Replayable source + durable checkpoint + idempotent/transactional sink.
Watermark purpose?
Bounds lateness so event-time aggregation state can be evicted; prevents unbounded state.
DABs vs Terraform?
Terraform provisions workspace infra; DABs packages/deploys code, jobs, and pipelines across environments.
Jobs vs All-Purpose compute cost?
Jobs compute is much cheaper per DBU (ephemeral, single-purpose); All-Purpose is interactive/shared.
MLflow aliases vs stages?
UC aliases (@champion/@challenger) are flexible pointers replacing fixed Staging/Production stages; enable clean promotion and A/B.
Which system tables for cost/audit?
system.billing.usage, system.billing.list_prices, system.access.audit.

13.4 Mosaic AI / Intelligence Platform

These questions reflect Databricks' 2025–2026 strategic direction. Expect at least 2–3 of these in a current FDE interview loop.

What is Mosaic AI and why is it relevant to an FDE?
Testing: platform awareness.
Mosaic AI is Databricks' AI layer: Foundation Model APIs, Vector Search, AI/BI Genie, Lakehouse Monitoring, and Databricks Apps — all governed by Unity Catalog. FDEs must know it because customers increasingly ask for AI capabilities on their existing lakehouse data, not just ETL pipelines.
Design a RAG chatbot over a customer's internal knowledge base using Databricks.
Testing: end-to-end AI architecture synthesis.
Ingest documents into a Delta bronze table → clean and chunk text in silver via DLT → create a Vector Search Delta Sync index (TRIGGERED, BGE embeddings) → at query time retrieve top-K chunks by similarity search → call DBRX Instruct or an external model via FMAPI with context → deploy as a Streamlit app via Databricks Apps. All governed by UC RBAC. Cost: pay-per-token (no always-on serving).
Pay-per-token vs Provisioned Throughput for Foundation Model APIs?
Testing: cost/architecture judgement.
Pay-per-token: no idle cost, scales instantly, right for dev/test or <~500K tokens/day. Provisioned Throughput: guaranteed tokens/sec, reserved capacity, right when daily volume is consistently high or you have strict latency SLA. Always model daily token volume before recommending PT.
A business analyst wants to query sales data in plain English. What do you set up?
Testing: Genie awareness + speed to value.
AI/BI Genie Space: point it at gold-layer UC tables, write business instructions (KPI definitions, date conventions, default filters), add verified SQL functions for complex metrics. Inherits UC RBAC so row-filters apply. Set it up in a day — ideal week-1 quick win that builds stakeholder trust while you build the proper data model.
How does Lakehouse Monitoring differ from custom Great Expectations pipelines?
Testing: DQ tooling positioning.
Lakehouse Monitoring is first-class UC: attach a monitor to a Delta table, it auto-produces profile_metrics and drift_metrics tables on a schedule — no code to maintain, lineage tracked, alertable. GE needs custom pipeline code and separate orchestration. Recommend LHM for standard statistical DQ on Delta; GE for highly custom assertion logic or existing GE investments.

13.5 How to Use This Bank

  • Drill out loud. Reading isn't enough — verbalize each answer until it's fluent and ~45–60 seconds long.
  • Attach a story. For behavioral questions, pre-write 4–5 STAR stories you can flex to multiple prompts.
  • Quantify everything. "Cut runtime from 6h to 45m and nodes from 20 to 4 (75% cost)" beats "made it faster."
  • Admit tier limits honestly. Saying "that needs a paid workspace; on Community I'd demonstrate it with X" builds credibility.
  • Loop back to the chapters. Each chapter's own Q&A section adds ~60 more questions with deeper answers.
